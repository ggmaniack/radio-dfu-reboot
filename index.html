Before you start

Power your radio
from USB

A browser-based tool for EdgeTX and OpenTX transmitters. No app installation needed — your browser talks directly to the radio's bootloader via WebUSB.

EdgeTX & OpenTX radios only Works with RadioMaster Pocket, Zorro, TX16S, and similar transmitters. Not for flight controllers.
Disable internal TX module first High-power ELRS modules (e.g. Boxer ELRS) draw too much current. Turn yours off in EdgeTX model settings before connecting.
Chrome or Edge required WebUSB is not supported in Firefox or Safari. Use a Chromium-based browser.
Windows: USB driver swap needed We'll walk you through replacing the default STM32 DFU driver with WinUSB so the browser can access your radio.

Use at your own risk. If anything breaks, that's on you.

01
Step 1 of 4

Connect your radio

  1. 1 Make sure your radio is completely off
  2. 2 Plug a USB cable into the top data port on your radio
  3. 3 Connect the other end to a direct USB port on your PC — avoid hubs if possible
  4. 4 The radio will power on with a blank screen — this is bootloader (DFU) mode. Correct.

USB-C to USB-C not detecting? Try a USB-A to USB-C cable — some radios have the CC lines miswired.

02
Step 2 of 4 — Windows

Fix the USB driver

Windows auto-installs its own STM32 DFU driver, which the browser can't use. Swap it to WinUSB using one of these tools:

Easiest

ImpulseRC Driver Fixer

Run it once — it automatically finds the STM32 BOOTLOADER device and replaces the driver.

Download Driver Fixer

Zadig (manual)

Open Zadig → select STM32 BOOTLOADER from the device list → choose WinUSB → click Install Driver.

Open Zadig →

If you have a Thrustmaster wheel, its drivers may conflict. Try a different USB port or machine.

Step 2 of 4

No driver needed

macOS and Linux don't need any driver changes. Your browser can access the radio directly.

You're all set — continue to the next step.

Radio is USB-powered

Your radio has exited DFU and is booting normally. Keep it plugged in — it'll run on USB power.

Keep in mind

  • Disable the internal TX module in your radio's model settings — it cuts power draw significantly and prevents brown-outs
  • Use a powered USB hub or a port directly on your computer for better current delivery
  • The radio may reboot or shut off if the USB port can't supply enough current
